<summary>Answer</summary>

### Question 2: Cost of Vagueness

**According to the book, how much time can a 15-minute collaborative spec save compared to vague prompts?**

B) 30 minutes

### Question 6: Evals-First Principle

### Question 8: Non-Goals

**Why are Non-Goals important in specifications?**

**B) They prevent scope creep and clarify boundaries**

Non-Goals explicitly state what the feature does NOT do, preventing over-engineering and misunderstandings.

### Question 12: Skills Definition

**What are Skills in the context of reusable intelligence?**

A) Developer programming abilities  
B) Horizontal expertise for 2-4 decision points  
C) Vertical specialization for complex domains  
D) Training programs for teams

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Horizontal expertise for 2-4 decision points**

Skills provide reusable guidance for recurring patterns with 2-4 decisions (e.g., input validation, error handling).

### Question 13: Subagents Definition

**What are Subagents in the context of reusable intelligence?**

A) Junior developers on the team  
B) Automated testing tools  
C) Vertical specialization for 5+ decision points with autonomous reasoning  
D) AI assistants for code review

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Vertical specialization for 5+ decision points with autonomous reasoning**

Subagents handle complex domains requiring deep expertise and autonomous reasoning (e.g., security review, performance optimization).
</details>

### Question 14: Skills vs Subagents

**When should you create a Skill instead of a Subagent?**

A) When you have 5+ decision points  
B) When you need autonomous reasoning  
C) When you have 2-4 decision points and horizontal patterns  
D) When the domain is very complex

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) When you have 2-4 decision points and horizontal patterns**

Skills are for simpler, horizontal patterns. Subagents are for complex, vertical specialization.
</details>

### Question 15: P+Q+P Pattern

**What does P+Q+P stand for?**

A) Plan, Query, Produce  
B) Persona, Questions, Principles  
C) Prepare, Qualify, Perform  
D) Process, Quality, Performance

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Persona, Questions, Principles**

P+Q+P (Persona + Questions + Principles) is the pattern for designing reusable intelligence that activates reasoning.

### Question 16: Why P+Q+P Works

**Why does the P+Q+P pattern activate reasoning instead of just pattern matching?**

Questions activate reasoning by prompting AI to consider multiple factors and make informed decisions.
